Your Journey

Recovery isn't a straight line. It has ups and downs. Keep moving forward.

Recognizing the Signs

Learn to notice it early. Pause. Regain control.

  • Physical

    Headaches, muscle tension, fatigue, or changes in sleep patterns.

  • Emotional

    Feeling overwhelmed, irritable, anxious, or lacking motivation.

  • Behavioral

    Withdrawing from friends, changes in appetite, or difficulty concentrating.

Daily Reminders

Tap to read common truths we often forget.

  • Growth isn't linear

    Some days you will feel like you are moving backward. That is part of the journey. Be patient with your progress.

  • This feeling will pass

    Emotions are temporary guests. They visit, they stay for a while, and then they leave. You have survived 100% of your bad days so far.

  • Rest is productive

    You cannot pour from an empty cup. Taking time to recharge is not "lazy," it's essential maintenance for your brain.

  • Focus on control

    You cannot control everything that happens to you, but you can control how you respond to it.

Healing is not an overnight process, it is a daily commitment to yourself.

Coping Strategies

Small changes can make a big difference.

  • Deep Breathing (4-7-8)

    Technique: Inhale through your nose for 4 seconds, Hold your breath for 7 seconds, and Exhale slowly through your mouth for 8 seconds. See the animation at the top of the page for practice.

  • Grounding Techniques

    Look around and name: 5 things you see, 4 you feel, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, and 1 you taste.

  • Journaling

    Writing down your thoughts can help clarify feelings and reduce the intensity of emotions.
